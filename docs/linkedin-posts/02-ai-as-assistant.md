# LinkedIn Draft 02: AI As Assistant

## Audience

Hiring managers and CTOs interested in how engineers use AI without handing over judgment.

## Draft

I used AI while building this Go repo, but I did not use it as the architect. I used it the way I would use a fast pair: draft a test name, compare two explanations, tighten a README paragraph, or ask whether a workflow boundary still sounded vague.

That worked best when the jobs were small and the constraints were already written down. The project had to use synthetic data, avoid live Banner, payment, and CRM claims, keep approvals with staff, and prove behavior with tests.

The most useful AI output was not code volume. It was friction removal. I could get a rough version faster, then spend my attention on the product questions: what action is safe, what gets logged, and when the assistant should stop and route to staff.

That is the AI workflow I trust. Small assistive tasks, explicit constraints, and a human still owning the result.
